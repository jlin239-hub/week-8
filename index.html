Week 8: Environmental Politics — Why Policy Can Fail After It “Passes”

Date: March 4, 2026 Reading: Miller, Chapter 24 Topic: Environmental Politics

Weekly reflection responding to a discussion question, with solutions, tradeoffs, gaps, and a final question.

Reflection on Miller Chapter 24

Chapter 24 made it clear that climate action isn’t blocked by a lack of solutions as much as by how policy actually gets made and enforced. Miller describes environmental policy as laws, regulations, and programs carried out by government agencies, and he lays out a policy life cycle: problem recognition, formulation, implementation, and adjustment (monitoring/evaluation). What hit me is what happens after a law passes: budgets have to be approved, agencies write the regulations, and then the whole thing can get weakened by fragmentation, lobbying, and court fights. He also warns about “foxes in charge of the henhouse” and the “revolving door effect,” where industries influence regulators from the inside. That’s why long-term issues like climate can suffer in democracies that reward short-term thinking.
